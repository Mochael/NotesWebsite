Travel

On this page I talk about my favorite experiences travelling as well as things I would recommend and things that I would like to do going back. Check out my youtube channel to watch some videos I put together of my travels.

San Francisco, CA

I visited San Francisco in early January 2020. While there I explored the city as well as the surrounding areas like Mountain View and Sunnyvale. During my trip I was lucky to have one of my friends chauffeur me around and show me all the coolest places to go to. I visited Stanford and Apple Park and biked over to Google's Campus. In San Francisco I got to visit Ghirardelli Square (and eat a massive ice cream sunday), take a ferry from Fisherman's Wharf around Alcatraz, ride the cable car across town, watch Hamilton (amazing), and eat delicious food throughout. My friend's family also was sure to stuff me fool of yummy traditional Korean cooking. I really enjoyed the trip, and my favorite part was a day long bike ride exploring Mountain View and its surrounding areas. The scenery in the more rural areas was breathtaking, and the weather was very nice despite it being in the middle of winter.

Madison, WI

I visited Madison, Wisconsin to interview with Epic Systems in late October 2019 (actually during Halloween). The city of Madison was quaint and beautiful, albeit freezing and snow-covered. I walked around the absurdly large University of Wisconsin Campus and came across many college students dressed for Halloween. The college seemed like a very fun and lively place to be, even if it was bitterly cold. The Epic Systems Office was breathtaking, and I doubt that I will ever see another office like it. Every employee had their own private office in a massive company campus where each building has its own theme ranging from Medieval Times to Star Wars. There wasn't enough time during the day I visited to see the whole office and interview, so I stayed a little late and ran around the campus trying to take in as much as I could. I spent most of my time in the Harry Potter section of the campus. There were several cafeterias around the campus with delicious food made by chefs employed by Epic who were tasked with not only making the food but ordering the ingredients and planning the menus. It seemed like a very high percentage of people in Madison would be working for Epic.
